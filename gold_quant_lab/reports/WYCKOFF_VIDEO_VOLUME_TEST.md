# Mind Math Money — "All Wyckoff Trading Secrets in 73 Min" — Reviewed & Tested

Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bUk2essMH2A (Mar 2025). A solid, free complete
Wyckoff course. (YouTube blocked the transcript; analysis is from the full chapter list.)

## What it teaches
Wyckoff's three laws (supply/demand, cause & effect, effort vs result), the market cycle
(accumulation → markup → distribution → markdown), key events (PS, SC, AR, ST, **Spring**,
**Upthrust**), the Composite Operator (smart money), **volume analysis**, and a Spring/Upthrust
trading strategy.

## What's actually new vs everything we've tested
- **Spring = sweep below support → reversal up; Upthrust = sweep above resistance → reversal down.**
  This is exactly our CRT/sweep mechanics — already tested (coin-flip; ~breakeven with a daily-trend filter).
- **Trade Springs in accumulation / Upthrusts in distribution** = a phase/context filter, the same idea
  as the daily-bias filter we already validated.
- **The one genuinely new lever: VOLUME ("effort vs result").** ICT/CRT ignore volume; Wyckoff centres it.
  This is the only thing worth a fresh test.

## Test: does a Wyckoff volume filter improve our gold CRT?

| Variant | Trades | Win% | avg_R | PF |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline | 304 | 35.9% | −0.119 | 0.83 |
| + daily 12m momentum bias (best so far) | 133 | 39.1% | **−0.016** | 0.98 |
| + high-volume displacement | 151 | 30.5% | −0.256 | 0.67 |
| + high-volume sweep (absorption) | 143 | 32.9% | −0.246 | 0.66 |
| + low-volume sweep (no-supply) | 81 | 35.8% | −0.081 | 0.88 |
| + bias + high-volume displacement | 58 | 31.0% | −0.223 | 0.71 |

**Important caveat:** Dukascopy gold "volume" is **tick volume, not real volume**, so this is an
indicative proxy. Real Wyckoff volume analysis needs a centralized-volume market (equities/futures/crypto).

## Verdict
- **No improvement on gold.** Requiring high volume on the sweep/displacement makes it *worse*
  (−0.25R) — plausibly because high-tick-volume sweeps on gold are often genuine news breakouts that
  *continue*, the opposite of a reversal. The only mildly-better volume variant (low-volume "no-supply"
  sweep, −0.081) still trails the simple daily-bias filter (−0.016).
- **Nothing mechanically new survives.** The video's edge ingredients reduce to (a) sweep reversals
  (already tested) and (b) phase/trend context (already the best lever, via daily bias).
- **Where volume *might* matter:** on a real-volume market. If we ever test this family on equities or
  crypto (real volume), the effort-vs-result filter deserves a re-run — it's untestable fairly on gold tick data.

## Net
The daily-bias filter (from tradingwyckoff.com) remains the only real CRT improvement, and it reaches
~breakeven, not profit. This Wyckoff course adds excellent *understanding* but no new measurable edge for
gold. The durable edge is still trend/momentum.
